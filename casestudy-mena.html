The Challenge

A blank canvas with high stakes

No existing product, no design system, no established workflows. Everything had to be designed from first principles. A misplaced decimal or unclear confirmation state isn't a UX issue — it's a financial risk.

Design System

Built to scale before the first screen

I built a comprehensive design system — typography, color tokens, spacing, component library, interaction patterns — handling light and dark contexts, dense data tables, and form-heavy workflows.

A design system for a trading platform isn't about making buttons look the same. It's about making sure a "confirm" button always looks like a confirm button.

— Design rationale

Key Flows

Wallet & Funding

Trading Interface

Portfolio Overview

At-a-glance performance — charts, allocation breakdowns, and P&L made scannable.

Reporting & Statements

Monthly statements, tax documents, and transaction exports for clients and compliance officers.

Account Management

Profile settings, security, linked banks, and notifications — the foundation that makes everything trustworthy.

Design Decisions

What I chose — and what I didn't

Decision 1 — Density vs. Clarity

Progressive disclosure: clean default view, but power users can expand panels, add columns, and customize layout. One interface, two levels of depth.

Decision 2 — Confirmation Friction

Two-step model: preview → confirm. Fast enough for experienced traders, safe enough for everyone else.
